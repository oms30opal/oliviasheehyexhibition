My Purpose

“Femme Life Exhibition” aims to present everything big and small about a girl’s
life and everything and anything about girl culture. This exhibition shines a
light on the light-hearted, carefree moments, whether it be whispering a secret into
the ear of your best friend, picking flowers for a bouquet, trying out a new
makeup look, or even window shopping on a Friday afternoon in the mall. In my goal
to represent girls and women, I included works of both real and fictional girls
and women. I included these fictional females because I believe they play a part
in representing and empowering the female viewer.

I believe in representing women of a wide range of shapes, sizes, and colors in
order to display the diversity that encapsulates the world. Every girl and woman
deserves to have a piece of media where they can admire a positive depiction of
someone who looks like them.

While I acknowledge that there is no shortage of women who look like me
represented in the media (white skin with brown hair), as a kid, I consumed a lot of
media geared towards my male counterparts. This meant that these media pieces
mainly featured a cast of male characters, with maybe one or two female side
characters, more often than not there to serve as a mom or a love interest. While I
did enjoy the episodes, I felt myself wanting to see more girls featured in a
positive manner. Even though children’s media has shifted its gears a lot compared
to a decade ago, my purpose still lives on. I want all girls and women to feel
represented through this exhibition, and give them a chance to see some like them
being featured in the spotlight.
